FOO BAR Farmer Jns has a huge number of cows. Earlier this year he ran an experiment where he gave 10 cows a special diet that he had heard could make them produce more milk. He recorded the number of liters of milk from these diet cows and from 15 normal cows during one month. This is the data:

diet_milk <- c(651, 679, 374, 601, 401, 609, 767, 709, 704, 679)
normal_milk <- c(798, 1139, 529, 609, 553, 743, 151, 544, 488, 555, 257, 692, 678, 675, 538)

Jns now wants to know: Was the diet any good, does it results in better milk production?

Here the effectiveness of special diet over the normal diet can be distinguished by modeling the diet as a normal distribution. Since values of the milk are varying a lot. So One to do is to standardize the data before sending it to stan.

sims.a = data.frame(as.matrix(fit.stan.a))
sims.b = data.frame(as.matrix(fit.stan.b))
summary.df = rbind(sims.a %>% do(data.frame(y = "diet", min = quantile(.$mu, 
    0.05), max = quantile(.$mu, 0.95), x = mean(.$mu))), (sims.b %>% do(data.frame(y = "normal", 
    min = quantile(.$mu, 0.05), max = quantile(.$mu, 0.95), x = mean(.$mu)))))
sims.diff = data.frame(x = sims.a$mu - sims.b$mu)
ggplot(summary.df) + geom_errorbarh(aes(x = x, xmin = min, xmax = max, y = y, 
    color = y)) + geom_point(aes(x = x, y = y, color = y))

ggplot() + geom_density(data = sims.a, aes(x = mu, color = "diet")) + geom_density(data = sims.b, 
    aes(x = mu, color = "normal"))

Looking at the posterior distribution of the differences in the mean:

data.frame(x=sims.a$mu - sims.b$mu) %>%
ggplot() + geom_density(aes(x = x)) +
    geom_vline(mapping=aes(xintercept=mean(x), color='mean'), alpha = 0.5) +
    geom_vline(mapping=aes(xintercept=median(x), color='median'), alpha =0.5)

data.frame(x=sims.a$mu - sims.b$mu) %>% summarize(mean(x), median(x))
